Obiter

AGLC4, applied automatically.

A free, open-source Microsoft Word add-in that handles AGLC4 citation formatting so you can focus on your argument. Insert footnotes, generate bibliographies, and validate references — all without leaving your document.

From obiter dictum — a remark in passing. Citations are the remarks that support the argument. Also sounds like “oh, better” — which is what your citations become.

What Obiter Does

Full AGLC4 Coverage

Why does Obiter exist?

AGLC4 was drafted without any model of its software implementation. The result is years of workarounds, broken tools, and unmet needs.

The official AGLC4 style hasn’t been updated since 2020

  • The Zotero CSL style was deliberately scoped to Australian cases and legislation only — it does not attempt to handle anything else.
  • International materials, treaties, and Hansard have zero tool support across every existing citation manager.
  • Two AI-generated community patches now circulate on GitHub because the official style cannot produce the dual citations that Australian courts require.

Existing tools cover about 30% of AGLC4

  • The UTS EndNote style requires manual cross-reference insertion for every subsequent reference — every (n X) must be hand-entered and refreshed with F9 before submission.
  • Parallel citations, required by every Australian court, cannot be produced by any existing tool.
  • Universities are publishing divergent institutional workarounds: Macquarie’s LibGuide tells users to ignore an entire section of the official UTS guidance.

AGLC5 is being drafted without implementer input

  • The AGLC5 Committee is in drafting following its 2023–2025 public consultation period.
  • No public submission has yet advocated for software-first design of citation rules.
  • Obiter has published an open letter requesting early access to draft rules so that the fifth edition can be the first one designed with implementability as a constraint.

How It Works

Three steps from installation to your first properly formatted citation.

1

Install

Add Obiter to Microsoft Word from AppSource. It appears as a task pane alongside your document, ready when you are.

2

Insert Citation

Select a source type, fill in the fields, and click Insert. Obiter formats the footnote and inserts it at your cursor position.

3

Write

Continue writing. Obiter manages ibid, cross-references, and short titles as your document grows. Generate a bibliography when you are done.
